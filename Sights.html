3 Amareswara Temple Amaravathi The most prominent and famous temple, is one of the Pancharama Kshetras, on the banks of river Krishna. Lord Indra installed the Shiva linga here. Temple is devoted to Lord Shiva, the 'Destroyer of the Universe'.The 15 feet high white marble Shivalinga is unique.You need to climb steps to see the upper portion. The towering gopuras in typical Dravidian style architecture are beautiful to watch. There are many inscriptions narrating glory of the temple. Click to navigate

5 Kotappakonda 42WP+Q27, Kotappakonda Rd, Narasaraopeta, Andhra Pradesh 522549 The temporal abode of Trikoteswara swamy, is 25kms from Guntur. Kotappa Konda hill appears with 3 peaks in any direction. Therefore, it is called “Trikoota Hills” and the God seated herein is famous as Trikoteswara Swamy. The popular three peaks derive their name from Hindu mythology namely Brahma,Vishnu and Maheswara. A steep flight of steps lead to the hill. The temple which is at a height of 600 feet is said to be in existence even before 1172 A.D. it offers a panoramic view of majestic hills. Dont miss the multiple statues enroute. Click to navigate
